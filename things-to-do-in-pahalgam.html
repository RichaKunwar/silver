Mamal Temple

According to the Sources, this is the temple where Ganesha was placed as door keeper by Parvati, not allowing anyone to enter the premises without her permission. This is the place where Shiva cut the head of Ganesha and gave him an elephant head. Mammal means don't go, and thus is also Known as Mammal Temple

King Jayasimha is believed to have constructed the Temple in the 4th century AD. It is a small temple, measuring only about 8 square feet internally. A shrine at the temple has a Shivlingam, a representation of Lord Shiva. The shrine is embellished with gold and silver embellishments.

The temple is a renowned tourist attraction for both pilgrims and visitors. It is especially crowded during the Shivratri Festival, which is celebrated every year in February or March. The Temple is located about 1 mile from Pahalgam Town.It is accessible by walking or getting a cab. From sunrise to sunset, the temple is open to the public.

Shri Amarnath Caves

The cave is believed to have been discovered by a Muslim sheperd names Buta Malik in the 15th century. According to legend, Malik was grazing his sheep in the area when he saw a pair of pigeons flying towards the cave. He followed the pigeons into the cave and saw the ice stalagmite. Malik believed that the stalagmite was a sign from God and he began to worship it.

The cave is now a popular pilgrimage site for Hindus. The Annual Amarnath Yatra, or pilgrimage to Amarnath, takes place in the summer months when the ice stalagmite is visible.

The yatra is a challenging journey that requires pilgrims to hike for several days through the mountains.However the journey is considered to be a very rewarding experience.

Chandanwari

Chandanwari is a popular destination for hiking and camping. Snow-capped mountains and beautiful greenery surround the meadow. The Lidder River runs across the meadow, creating a picturesque background for the activities.

The meadow is also the starting point for the Amarnath Yatra, a Hindu pilgrimage to the Amarnath Cave. The cave is located about 32 kilometres (20 miles) from Chandanwari

It is a Popular spot for bird watching. Over 100 species of birds have been spotted in the area.

Aru Valley

It is a beautiful valley Located in the Pahalgam district of Jammu and Kashmir, India. The valley has multiple hiking routes, including one to the Kolahoi Glacier. There are several hiking trails in the valley including the trek to the Kolahoi Glacier. The valley is also a popular camping and picnic spot.

Here are some interesting Facts about Aru Valley:
The Valley also helps rare and endangered plants and animals to survive

The Valley is an excellent location for bird viewing. Over 100 bird species have been spotted nearby..

The Valley is a popular destination for photographers. The stunning scenery makes for some great photos.

If you're searching for a lovely and peaceful spot to walk, camp, or relax, Aru Valley is the place to be. .

Betaab Valley

Betab Valley is a scenic valley found in Jammu and Kashmir's Pahalgam district. It is famous for its beautiful green meadows, snow-capped mountains, and pure streams. The valley was named after the Bollywood movie “Bobby” which was filmed here in the 1970s.

The Valley is surrounded by the Pir Panjal mountain range. This valley is a home to the Betab river which is a tributary of the Lidder River. The valley is an excellent place to go bird viewing. Over 100 bird species have been sighted in the vicinity. It is a popular destination for photographers. The stunning scenery makes for some great photos.

If you are looking for a beautiful and serene place to hike, camp or simply relax, then betab valley is the perfect place for you.
